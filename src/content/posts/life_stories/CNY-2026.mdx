## 🥟 New Year's Eve · Making Dumplings

On New Year's Eve, we—**Luo Yaosheng, Zhou Zixiang, Li Juxing, Wang Zixuan, and Chen Junfei**—went to Tesco Extra to buy a bunch of ingredients, and then went back to the dorm to make dumplings in a lively atmosphere.

Talking and laughing, the room was full of the smell of cooking and life, it's a great memory.

The funniest part was **Zixuan**. Every time he was in charge of rolling out the dumpling wrappers, he would yell:

> *"You rolled it too long! And it's so weird! Can someone help me wipe my ass!"*

Hahahahahaha. Zixiang and I just looked at each other—after all, the two of us were in charge of wrapping the dumplings, leaving us speechless for a moment 😂

## 💭 Some Reflections

After this gathering, I felt like it had been a long time since I chatted freely with friends like this, and I really missed the days before school started—back then, we participated in various activities every day, talking and laughing with friends.

After school started, everyone became busy with their own things, and gradually built up their own "nearby" social circles. This is actually very normal, socializing is inherently based on proximity, people look for suitable friends based on convenience. But not entirely—some friends, even if it's inconvenient to meet, will specifically arrange to hang out because they truly hit it off.

> Sometimes being simpler: just focusing on how you get along with your friends, allowing each other to have their own circles, and maintaining a certain social boundary, can actually make the relationship lighter.

---

One more thing, this time I **truly felt the meaning of the phrase "Wishing a Happy Spring Festival to Chinese all over the world"**.

Being in a foreign land, hearing this phrase brought tears to my eyes. In the past, celebrating the New Year with family in China, I felt absolutely nothing when hearing this phrase.
