## Musings

Lately, I feel like many things have been lingering in my mind, so I decided to write them all down using the most authentic words. Following my usual pattern, I'll first record the current time.

> **Current time: London 2026.2.12 18:06**

I'm sitting alone at the desk in my small room, listening to emo songs playing on NetEase Cloud, facing my computer to organize my thoughts, trying to write down every thought and emotion in my head.

Before I knew it, I have been in the UK for **141 days**. Looking back at the bits and pieces of these days, I feel that time really passes too quickly, and I feel like there are still so many things I haven't had time to do. If you really ask me, what exactly haven't I done? Hmm... it's hard to describe, it just feels like in my imagination, my life in the UK should be a balance of life and study, studying hard on one hand, while actively participating in various social activities and integrating into the foreigners' circle on the other.

But what about the reality? Although I admit that I have been attending classes properly, I found that most of my knowledge still comes from self-study, and most of the friends around me are still Chinese. All of this seems to be somewhat different from what I envisioned. As for my English proficiency, there's no such thing as sudden leaps and bounds just because I came to the UK, and I didn't become more native-like. But I must admit, my English thinking or proficiency is definitely stronger than the previous me, after all, I've participated in quite a few group discussions.

### The Importance of Reflection

I think if I don't stop and think about what I'm doing now, or where I am, I feel a bit numb. I always believe that **regular reflection and introspection can help you get back on the right track you want to walk on in time**.

Actually, a long time ago, I told myself that I wanted to keep blogging and recording, and I even secretly told myself, how hard could it be, posting two or three articles a week, or at worst, one article a week should be manageable. But the truth is that I haven't posted a single one until now, until today when I decided to really sit down and have a good conversation with myself.

But actually, this is inseparable from some quotes and experiences shared by a blogger named **Dan Koe** that I saw on Twitter recently, which touched me a lot.

### On "Self-Discipline"

I often think about the word self-discipline, and of course, I've discussed it with many people, and I know that practicing many things in the long term is very difficult. When you are already on track, you might not feel it, but for outsiders, for those who are trying to step into the circle of "self-discipline", there is still a certain level of difficulty.

I don't want to overemphasize self-discipline because I feel that self-discipline, for people who just started practicing, is a mechanical and task-oriented mode, forcing themselves to execute a specific behavior, such as getting up early to run, working out, sleeping and waking early, reading, etc. But when I look back at the times I regularly practiced these things, I didn't feel that I was disciplining myself. I just felt that, **I wanted to do these things, and doing them felt very comfortable**.

But looking back now, when my life is disrupted by some messy things, leading me to perhaps be unable to do those things regularly, I feel that trying to restart doing them like before is really difficult. But why? My mindset before clearly wasn't like this; I didn't need to force myself to do these things diligently as if following some routine. My inner self just told me I wanted to do it, and I even felt uncomfortable if I didn't.

- When I stick to sleeping early and getting up early, and my spirit becomes very good, I feel very comfortable.
- When I stick to working out and see my body shape remains good, and my abs and chest muscles are still there, I feel very comfortable.

**This positive feedback makes me not need to force myself to do them; I just naturally want to do them.**

### On Procrastination

Also, I found that I am really procrastinating sometimes. Starting from last semester, I wanted to develop a website for managing to-dos and tracking progress. I've finished 60%-70% of it, but because I encountered some obstacles later, many fields were messed up globally, causing problems in many places, and the task flow couldn't run well, so I stalled.

I originally had this idea because I found that many to-do lists on the market couldn't manage tasks well; most of them can only manage the completion of tasks, but can't well calculate the status of tasks. For example, I would prefer to know how much time I spent on a specific task to better know my task completion efficiency, rather than simply checking the task to indicate it's completed. So I added timing, task management, and visual analysis to the website, so that I can clearly manage every task.

I now know my problem. I will try to clarify it, then formulate a plan to overcome it, conquer it step by step, and then finally launch it officially. Of course, this is just my assumption, and I am not sure if I have enough energy and time to realize it, but I think, if it really comes true, I want to try posting it on Xiaohongshu, and then let everyone test it and give me feedback so that I can continue to improve.

### Recent Emotions

Also, my recent mood has been a bit low. I think it's also quite important to constantly try different states and find the state I truly like. **If you can easily switch between different states, that's also a skill**.

### Anxiety and Opportunities in the AI Era

Speaking of this, I thought of the anxiety brought to me by the rapid development of AI recently. As a computer science student, this is both a challenge and an opportunity. I realized that traditional website development has a hard time having long-term development. Although from the current point of view, AI still can't develop some huge enterprise-level projects and can only complete so-called functions, I firmly believe that following the current trend, these won't be problems.

**On Feb 11, Bytedance released Seedance 2.0**, which also sparked fierce discussions on Twitter. By just giving it reference images and videos, it can perfectly replicate the same camera movements, which is a huge impact on self-media or the advertising industry. What used to require many cameramen, editors, and lighting engineers to complete a piece of work, now only needs a product image and an accurate prompt to achieve most of the effects you want. Now video editing can also be achieved by corresponding AI (chatcut).

> **I very much agree with a sentence: In today's era of booming AI development, we should cultivate an ability, which is the adaptability to face changes.**

Wake up from a sleep, and maybe a tech company has released a model that can replace the field you worked on before. At this time, what is tested is our adaptability. How to turn challenges into opportunities is very important, which really tests a person's perspective and pattern of seeing problems.

## Problems Encountered Now

### Problem 1: Lack of Attention

I feel that my attention is getting worse and worse, because a large amount of time is soaked in fragmented information and short videos, leading me to now even find it hard to attentively finish reading a ten-minute article. **The lack of focus actually greatly affects learning ability**, because I can't attentively read the explanation of a concept or understand what a piece of code actually implements. When I force myself to understand, I find that I also need to read it repeatedly many times or stare at a piece of code for a long time to "take it in".

### Problem 2: Over-reliance on AI

Besides, I am now a bit heavily reliant on AI. I should give myself more space to think. When encountering problems, I should think first, rather than brainlessly sending it directly to AI, otherwise my brain will become more and more dull.

In today's AI era, one of the most special traits of humanity is **emotional traits**. When we think about a matter or make a decision, we have a comprehensive consideration of the perception of the real world and the situation we are in; we will think more, more divergently, and have more "human flavor".
