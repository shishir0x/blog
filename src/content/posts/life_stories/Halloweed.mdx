### Halloween



***Here comes my first life blog post***

Thursday night (10.30) was my only Halloween carnival night. I went to the college's formal. The price was pretty good, £10, with starters, main courses, and desserts. I'll show you guys later; before that, I have to talk about the preparation (which was basically no preparation).

First of all, a big thank you to Sister L for her generous sponsorship as follows:

Thank you so much! Originally, I didn't plan to prepare anything because classes ended at 18:00 that day, and the formal was at 19:30. I was thinking of finding a place to stay and wait for dinner, but I happened to be walking towards St John's with Sister L. I was muttering about how "plain" I looked, and Sister L said, "How about coming to my place to draw some Halloween elements," and then came the result below.

I don't know why, but while talking at the formal, my voice suddenly felt hoarse. The sky was falling. Even Xiaohan, who usually talks the most, became much quieter because she got sick, oh damn.

This meal was quite interesting; I can only say the world is too small. Sitting across from me was my neighbor, and our landlord is the exact same person—the boss of North Street 56.

She asked: Where do you live?

I said I live over at Gilesgate. She said: Oh, me too.

Then she asked if my landlord was Chinese.

I said: Yeah.

She asked again: The boss of North Street 56?

WTF, I said: Yeah.

Then we matched our house numbers: I'm 49, she's 42. I'm speechless, I can only say Durham is really too small.

She also shared with me that one of their roommates loves to cook, reports the menu every day, and has starters, main courses, and desserts. Oh my god!

She even invited me to eat together when there's a chance, which made me so happy.
